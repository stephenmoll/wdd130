Frequently Asked Questions

Safety

How long your waiting list?

Our waiting list can vary between 1-2 months. We advise booking 2 months in advance to avoid disapointment.

Do you do refunds?

For cancellations 3 or more weeks in advance we offer full refunds. For cancellations between 2-3weeks in advance we offer a half refund. Unfortunately, we can not offer any form of refund for cancellations under two weeks in advance.

Do the camping grounds have ablution facilities?

Yes, all our camping grounds have ablution facilities and running water.

What is your gear like?

We believe in style points. If you look good, you raft good, right? This is why our gear is not only new, but top of the line. Why give you a helmet, PFD (personal floatation device), wet-suit, or splash top that we wouldn't choose ourselves..? That's just silly.

Can I bring my own gear?

Most helmets and home PFD's do not fit our stringent regualations for safety. If you have questions about specific pieces of whitewater specific apparel, please contact (see contact page).

Will I fall out of the raft?

Will you get wet? Yes. Will you fall out? Potentially. Will you swim all the way to the ocean? No.

In the event that you do fall out of the raft, our highly experienced guides will direct you to the safest path and get you back in the boat rapidly. On class 4/5 river trips we have safety kayakers which act as swim coaches and additional rescue resources. At the minimum, our guides are trained in Advanced First Aid, Cardio Pulmonary Resuscitation and Swift Water Rescue.

Can I go rafting if I'm pregnant, have a back problem, or a preexisting health condition?

The best person to advise you on this, is your doctor and your body. We are comfortable taking you on any river trip as long as you are comfortable with it yourself.

Does the river go in a circle?

No. However, rest assured transportation to the river put in and take out is provided and we have yet to miss returning a person to their vehicles thus far.

What can I bring on the raft?

If you do not want to sacrifice it to the river- DO. NOT. BRING. IT. This includes but is not limited to: cell phones, waatches, glasses without a strap.

Can I bring alcohol?

Overnight clients are welcome to bring alcohol for their time at camp. For safety reasons, alcohol is not allowed before or during a river trip. For our clients safety, we may refuse service to anyone we believe is under the influence of drugs and/or alcohol.

What are the food options?

We are more than happy to accommodate those with food allergies and unique dietary preferences. If you have any dietary restrictions, we ask that you please notify us prior to the trip. Our lunch spreads typically call for a deli style lunch. Complete with an assortment of meats, cheeses, a whole lot of veggies, condiments galore, fresh fruit, chips, and cookies.

Is rafting safe?

As with any outdoor sport or adventure activity, rafting has it's potential inherent risks. Your safety is our number one priority both on & off the river. All members of our staff from the drivers to the guides are trained above the minimum standard, so you can rest easy knowing you are in good hands.

How are your guides trained?

All of our guides are extensively trained above the industry standard. All guides are required at the minimum to have current first aid, cpr, and swift water rescue training so you can rest assured knowing you are in good hands. We encourage guides to take boats out and continue their training on days off, join advanced trips as a paddle assist in a senior guides raft, and ensure we take the time to show how incredible.

What happens if there is an emergency?

All our guide groups carry with them a satelite phone should evacuation ever be required.

Are there wild animals?

Wild aninmals are part of being in nature, that being said, we take strict precautions to insure the safety of our fellow raftors. In areas known for bears we have bear-proof fencing around the camping areas. Our guides are also highly trained for the rare occasions wild animals are encountered.

Are there snakes?

Snakes, like all wild animals, are part of being in nature. That being said, they are really seen. Our guides will give you a snake and wild animal safety talk at the beginning of each trip. In the unlikely event of a snakebite, our guides are trained in first aid and have access to a satelite phone.

In the event that you do fall out of the raft, our highly experienced guides will direct you to the safest path and get you back in the boat rapidly. On class 4/5 river trips we have safety kayakers which act as swim coaches and additional rescue resources. At the minimum, our guides are trained in Advanced First Aid, Cardio Pulmonary Resuscitation and Swift Water Rescue.
